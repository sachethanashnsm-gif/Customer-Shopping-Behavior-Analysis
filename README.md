# 🛒 Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis

## 📌 Overview
An end-to-end data analytics project using **Python, PostgreSQL, and Power BI** to analyze the shopping habits of 3,900 retail customers. The goal is to uncover hidden purchasing patterns and provide data-driven recommendations to optimize marketing, inventory, and customer loyalty programs.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack
* **Python (Pandas, SQLAlchemy):** Data cleaning, feature engineering, and database pipeline integration.
* **PostgreSQL:** Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) using advanced SQL to extract core business metrics.
* **Power BI:** Interactive executive dashboard for dynamic data visualization.

## 🚀 Top Business Insights
* **Seasonality:** **Fall** is the most profitable season (highest average order value), while **Spring** drives the most foot traffic.
* **Inventory:** **Medium-sized** clothing in vibrant colors (Orange, Violet, Teal) heavily dominates sales volume.
* **The Loyalty Gap:** There are over 2,500 highly active repeat buyers who are *not* subscribed to the loyalty program. Furthermore, non-subscribers actually spend slightly more per transaction than active subscribers.
* **Regional Surprise:** **Montana** emerged as the top-performing market by revenue, outperforming traditionally massive states like California and Illinois.

## 📈 Strategic Recommendations
1. **Upsell in the Fall:** Focus marketing spend on premium, higher-margin items during the Fall season when customers are willing to spend more.
2. **Spring "Bundle" Deals:** Leverage the high foot traffic in Spring with multi-item discounts to force average cart values upward.
3. **Revamp Subscriptions:** Aggressively target the "Returning" customer tier with exclusive benefits to convert them into active subscribers.
4. **Smart Procurement:** Prioritize stock for Medium sizes in vibrant colors and reduce warehouse reliance on heavily discounted items like hats and sneakers.
